# Vietnam Legal and Tax Note

## Status

This document is a non-lawyer operational note. It is not legal advice, tax advice, accounting advice, or regulatory advice.

## Working interpretation

Current public reporting and common legal interpretation indicate:

- owning or trading crypto assets is not treated as an outright banned activity in itself;
- crypto assets are not recognized as legal tender or legal means of payment in Vietnam;
- using crypto as a payment instrument can create legal risk;
- Vietnam's digital-asset framework is evolving;
- tax/reporting obligations may arise when gains are realized, assets are converted, or funds are withdrawn into VND.

## Repo wording rule

Use:

```text
voluntary digital-asset donation
public transparency ledger
support campaign
disbursement report
```

Avoid:

```text
payment
legal tender
settlement currency
crypto banking
exchange service
investment fund
profit distribution
```

## Operational guardrails

Maintainers should:

1. keep wallet and transaction records;
2. keep source-of-funds notes where practical;
3. avoid handling money for others as a business;
4. avoid promising conversion or payment services;
5. document any VND conversion event;
6. consult a qualified Vietnamese lawyer/accountant before scaling.

## Tax note

If assets are converted to VND, sold, or otherwise realized, maintainers and recipients should retain records and handle applicable tax/reporting obligations.
