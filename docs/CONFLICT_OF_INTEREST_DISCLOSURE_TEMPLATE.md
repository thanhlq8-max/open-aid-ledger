# Conflict-of-Interest Disclosure Template

Use this template when a maintainer, reviewer, campaign proposer, or disbursement approver may have a personal, financial, family, business, or trading-community relationship with a campaign or beneficiary.

## Disclosure record

```text
date_utc:
disclosing_party:
role: maintainer | reviewer | proposer | beneficiary_referrer | other
campaign:
beneficiary_id_or_group:
relationship_type:
financial_interest: yes | no | unknown
prior_relationship: yes | no | unknown
trading_or_business_relationship: yes | no | unknown
recommended_action: recuse | disclose_and_continue | reject | needs_review
reviewer:
decision:
notes:
```

## Examples requiring disclosure

- A maintainer proposes support for a person they personally know.
- A reviewer has a business relationship with a campaign organizer.
- A trader-community donor requests preferential disbursement to a trading associate.
- A maintainer could benefit reputationally or financially from a campaign decision.
- A beneficiary is connected to a maintainer's family, company, or trading group.

## Recusal guidance

A person should recuse from approval when they:

- may receive funds directly or indirectly;
- have close personal ties to a beneficiary;
- have a financial relationship with the campaign organizer;
- cannot review the case independently;
- are involved in a dispute related to the campaign.

## Guardrails

Conflict disclosures must not expose sensitive beneficiary data. Use redacted beneficiary IDs where possible.

This template must not be used to justify:

- trading account top-up;
- margin-call support;
- copy trading;
- managed accounts;
- profit sharing;
- investment products;
- return promises.
