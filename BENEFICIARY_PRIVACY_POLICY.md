# Beneficiary Privacy Policy

## Principle

Transparency must not expose vulnerable people to harm.

Beneficiary identity should be protected by default.

## Do not publish

Do not publish:

- full legal names;
- home addresses;
- phone numbers;
- identity cards;
- passports;
- unredacted hospital documents;
- child photos without explicit guardian consent;
- exact school/home location;
- screenshots containing private messages;
- wallet screenshots containing secrets.

## Evidence levels

Recommended privacy levels:

| Level | Meaning |
|---|---|
| PUBLIC | Safe public proof with consent |
| REDACTED | Public proof with personal data removed |
| PRIVATE_REVIEW | Evidence reviewed by maintainers but not published |
| PARTNER_CONFIRMED | Trusted partner confirms support |
| ATTESTED | Maintainer attestation only |

## Children and vulnerable people

For children, homeless people, abuse survivors, and medical hardship cases, use `REDACTED`, `PRIVATE_REVIEW`, or `PARTNER_CONFIRMED` unless explicit consent exists.

## Takedown

If a beneficiary asks for public evidence to be removed, maintainers should remove or redact it unless there is a legal reason not to.
